Mark Twain's Biography

Mark Twain was born on November 30, 1835, in Florida, Missouri. He was the son of John and Olivia Langdon Clemens. Mark Twain's father was a surveyor. At the age of five, Twain was sent to live with his older brother, Henry, and his family in Hannibal, Missouri. Twain attended the Hannibal Academy and later attended the Keokuk College. After completing his education, Twain became a riverboat pilot. He sailed on the Mississippi River for 12 years and traveled to many places in the United States and Canada. Twain wrote many humorous books and articles, including "The Adventures of Tom Sawyer" and "The Adventures of Huckleberry Finn". Twain was a famous author and lecturer. He traveled all over the world and became a prominent figure in American literature and American culture. Twain died on April 21, 1910, in Redding, Connecticut.
